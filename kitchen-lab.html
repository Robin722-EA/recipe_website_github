How to clean a pan

Step 1

Go to your nearest nasty communal kitchen (preferably with traces of "secret" smoke sessions) and find your favorite pan that has been abused by fellow flat mates.

Step 2

Pick up said pan, and dump a but ton of water on it. Throw all the water out of the pan when you are done waterboarding it.

Step 3

Drop a bit of dish soap in the pan. Add a tiny hint of water (enough to make the soap soapy) and start scrubbing with a dish brush. When you are done dry brushing, rinse off the soap while brushing at the same time.

Step 4

If somehow the filth has not disappeared, pick up a metal sponge, add another drop of dish soap, and give it a good scrub. If you don't have a metal sponge because all of the pans you have would be damaged if you used it, then make sure you buy one. This is your new favorite pan, TAKE CARE OF IT!

Step 5

If, somehow, the pan is still dirty, try using other cleaning materials. Like Dasty, the world famous, only available in the Netherlands, degreaser. Spray it on the pan, leave it be for a bit and wipe it off with a paper towel.

Step 6

If after all of that it still hasn't worked out, then it is time to pull out the big guns! Grab your nearest bottle of bleach and dump it in the pan, add a large squirt of dish soap to the mixture and start scrubbing. You have a 50% chance it works! And if it doesn't, then maybe the paramedics can help as they inevitably bring you to the hospital for breathing in Chloramine gas.
